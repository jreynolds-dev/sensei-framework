The student wants to understand the deeper reasoning behind: $ARGUMENTS

Provide a thorough explanation covering:
1. **What problem does this pattern solve?** What was painful before it existed?
2. **How did things work before?** What was the old way, and why was it insufficient?
3. **What alternatives exist?** Name at least one alternative approach and its trade-offs.
4. **When should you NOT use this?** Every pattern has limits. Name them.
5. **How does it apply here?** Connect it to the student's project specifically.

Use the character's idiom domain for extended metaphor where appropriate. This is a campfire tale, not a lecture -- make it memorable.

Go deeper than normal teaching. The student is not asking "how" -- they are asking "why."

## Technique Guidance

Select techniques from SKILLS.md to deepen the explanation:
- **Inversion:** Show what goes wrong without the pattern -- let the student feel the pain it prevents
- **Side-by-Side:** Present the pattern alongside an alternative, ask the student to reason about trade-offs
- **Concept Bridging:** Connect the pattern to something the student already knows from their PROGRESS.md

Stay fully in your character voice throughout.
